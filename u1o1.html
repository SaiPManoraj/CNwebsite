1.1.4) Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

There are many risks that may impact digital projects. This could be as basic as your computer or digital device shutting down whilst you’re working. This may only cost you a minute or so whilst it reboots however, should you fail to save your work as you progress it could result in the loss of your work – something I’m sure we have all experienced at some point! Aside from this trivial example there are many others that can heavily impact digital projects. Whilst creating something digitally, we are forming certain assumptions. For example, we assume that the software will need a certain operating system. What happens when half way through the project, the operating system is updated and the current project won’t work on this updated software? It sets you back and could mean a delay in the project which could have costly implications. What happens when the company who produced the software you’re currently using releases an End of Life statement for the software stating that it will be discontinued. Even if you complete your project before this, will the digital project be useful still be useful a year after? If not, you may wish to reconsider the software being used. To mitigate these issues, it may be wise to plan ahead and to have backups for each potential risk from the start of the project to prevent time lost and panic midway through the project.

1.1.5) Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software – how do you select the right kind of technology for the job?

It is important to find the right systems and software for your target market. This could be designing a very basic app which sets alarms for OAPs to take their medication, or it could be a project to encrypt data to keep private files secure. Each of these two examples require very different software. Once you have narrowed down the potential software, then you can look at which one is less costly, more efficient, or is just preferred by the people in your team (since they have previous experience using it).

1.1.7) Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

New GDPR legislation (as of May 2018) has allowed a greater deal of transparency between people using websites and those collecting, storing and using the data of such people(i.e. data controllers and data processors). For those developing websites, it is important to bear in mind the impact of GDPR. This accounts for IP addresses, fingerprints, cookies, and any other system-generated data that identifies an individual.
